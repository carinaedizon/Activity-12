Bachelor in Computer Technology

The BACT is a four-year degree program that focuses on the practical aspects of the science of computing. The program develops core skills in multimedia technology, web/ mobile development and networks or systems administration. Furthermore, it prepares learners with supervisory and management skills. The curriculum is derived from the BS Industrial Technology major in Computer technology with CHED Certificate of Program Compliance (COPC) no. 31 series of 2021.

Program Objectives

To prepare learners with the practical skills in multimedia technology, web/mobile development, and network systems administration, and supervisory skills to manage an organization in these areas of competencies, engage in a startup business, or pursue a teaching career.

Specific Objectives

  1. Demonstrate understanding of the importance of being an ethical competing professional when making work-related decisions.
  2. Develop products or processes by applying their knowledge in computer or network systems, and development of productivity tools.
  3. Participate as an active member of a development service team with leadership roles when appropriate.
  4. Apply communication skills effectively to promote ideas, goals, processes, or products.
  5. Make positive contributions to the community with the skills and abilities learned in the Program.
  6. Pursue lifelong learning for personal and professional growth through varied experiential and field-based opportunities.
